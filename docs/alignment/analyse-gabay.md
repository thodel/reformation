## Short Summaries of Key Works

### 1 · Gabay (2025): Rien de Nouveau chez Rimbaud...

**Full Title:** «Rien de Nouveau chez Rimbaud : sur l’attribution des Illuminations» (Nothing of Nouveau in Rimbaud: on the Attribution of the Illuminations)
**In:** *Humanités numériques*, 11 | 2025.
**Keywords:** stylométrie, attribution d’auteur, stylistique, analyse textuelle, littérature, humanités numériques (stylometry, authorship attribution, stylistics, textual analysis, literature, digital humanities).

This article addresses the controversial claim by Eddie Breuil (2014) that Arthur Rimbaud's *Illuminations* were actually written by Germain Nouveau. Gabay refutes this reattribution by mobilizing computational methods for authorship attribution, specifically using support vector machines. The study argues that the *Illuminations* were indeed written by Rimbaud and further explores his style at an infralexical level by analyzing machine-generated results.

**Methodological Character:** Application of stylometry and machine learning (support vector machines) to a classical literary problem; interdisciplinary approach combining literary criticism and computational analysis to shift the "regime of proof" from manual collection of indices to systematic, data-driven methods.

### 2 · Gabay & Clérice (2024): The birth of French orthography...

**Full Title:** «The birth of French orthography. A computational analysis of French spelling systems in diachrony»
**In:** CHR2024 – Computational Humanities Research Conference, Aahrus, Denmark, Dec 2024.

This conference paper presents a computational analysis of French spelling systems across different historical periods (diachrony). It investigates the evolution and establishment of French orthography using quantitative methods.

**Methodological Character:** Computational historical linguistics; diachronic analysis of linguistic features; application of computational methods to understand the historical development of language systems.

### 3 · Gabay, Pinche, Christensen, Camps (2024): SegmOnto: A Controlled Vocabulary...

**Full Title:** «SegmOnto: A Controlled Vocabulary to Describe and Process Digital Facsimiles»
**In:** *Journal of Data Mining and Digital Humanities*, 2024.

This article introduces "SegmOnto," a controlled vocabulary designed to describe and process digital facsimiles. This is a crucial contribution to digital scholarly editing and digital humanities infrastructure, enabling more standardized and interoperable ways to work with digitized historical documents.

**Methodological Character:** Development of digital humanities infrastructure; standardization of metadata and descriptive practices for digital facsimiles; focus on interoperability and systematic processing of historical documents.

## Overall Assessment

Simon Gabay's scholarly profile is marked by a strong commitment to **computational humanities**, with a particular emphasis on applying quantitative methods to literary and linguistic history. His expertise spans:

1.  **Authorship Attribution:** Utilizing stylometry and machine learning to address long-standing questions in literary criticism (e.g., Rimbaud's *Illuminations*).
2.  **Historical Linguistics & Philology:** Analyzing the diachronic evolution of language, such as French orthography, and developing tools for classical texts.
3.  **Digital Infrastructure:** Contributing to the development of standards and vocabularies for processing digital facsimiles (`SegmOnto`).
4.  **Medieval Studies:** His monograph *L'Acteur au Moyen Âge* demonstrates a solid foundation in traditional historical and philological research in medieval contexts.

Gabay's work is interdisciplinary, bridging literary studies, linguistics, and computer science. He effectively employs advanced computational techniques to provide new insights into humanities questions, making him a key contributor to the evolving landscape of digital scholarship.
