# Analysis of Publications: Anne-Sophie Bories

Based on the bibliographic list and submitted PDFs for nomination in computational philology.

## Key Research Areas & Contributions
Anne-Sophie Bories' work primarily focuses on the intersection of literary studies, computational methods, and the analysis of poetry, particularly related to Raymond Queneau. Her research interests include:
*   Computational literary studies and stylistics.
*   The study of prosody, voice, and silence in poetry readings, especially through audio archives.
*   Humour studies, specifically in relation to food, taste, and cultural analysis in songs (e.g., Renaud).
*   Digital humanities methodologies applied to literary analysis.

Her contributions often involve interdisciplinary collaboration, particularly with Nils Couturier, and frequently engage with projects like PRIMA « Le Rire des vers ». She explores how computational tools can illuminate aspects of literary works that are traditionally analyzed through qualitative methods.

### 2. Book Chapter: The Polite Revolution of Computational Literary Studies
*   **Title:** The Polite Revolution of Computational Literary Studies
*   **Authors:** Bories, Anne-Sophie; Ruiz Fabo, Pablo; Plecháč, Petr
*   **Year:** 2023
*   **Published in:** *Computational Stylistics in Poetry, Prose, and Drama*, edited by Bories, Plecháč, and Ruiz Fabo, De Gruyter, pp. 1-18.
*   **Contribution Note:** 80% contribution as first author. This article reflects her engagement with the theoretical and methodological underpinnings of computational literary studies.
*   **Summary:** This chapter contributes to the field of computational literary studies, likely exploring its evolution and impact on the analysis of poetry, prose, and drama. As a transversal reflection, it sets the stage for the edited volume it introduces, highlighting interdisciplinary approaches.

### 3. Article: A thug’s grub: food and drink as status in Renaud’s songs
*   **Title:** A thug’s grub: food and drink as status in Renaud’s songs
*   **Authors:** Couturier, Nils; Bories, Anne-Sophie
*   **Year:** 2025 (forthcoming)
*   **Published in:** *Tasting funny? A (cook)book on humour and taste* (Special issue edited by Bories and Couturier), EJHR : European Journal of Humour Studies, Vol. 13 No. 1.
*   **Contribution Note:** 50% contribution. Part of the PRIMA project « Le Rire des vers ». This article showcases her interdisciplinary work, blending literary and cultural studies with humour studies through the lens of food and drink in popular music.
*   **Summary:** This co-authored article analyzes the semiotics of food and drink in Renaud's songs, exploring how these elements function as markers of status and contribute to humor. It is part of a special issue on humor and taste, demonstrating a unique application of cultural analysis.

### 4. Article (In Press): « Sur l’herbe je n’ai rien à dire. » : Raymond Queneau et la lecture de poésie
*   **Title:** « Sur l’herbe je n’ai rien à dire. » : Raymond Queneau et la lecture de poésie
*   **Authors:** Bories, Anne-Sophie; Couturier, Nils
*   **Year:** In press
*   **Published in:** *Archives sonores de poésie #2*, edited by Lang, Murat, Pardo, Presses du Réel.
*   **Contribution Note:** 50% contribution. Part of the PRIMA project « Le Rire des vers ». This article delves into Raymond Queneau's relationship with the spoken word and his own poetry readings.
*   **Summary:** This article explores Raymond Queneau's complex relationship with his own voice and the performance of his poetry. It analyzes radio and audiovisual recordings from 1948-1965, examining his use of prosody, silence, and his reluctant engagement with public readings. The research seeks to quantify these phenomena, particularly speech rate, to understand his unique vocal expression.

## Overall Assessment
Anne-Sophie Bories' work is characterized by a strong interdisciplinary approach, bridging traditional literary analysis with computational methods and cultural studies. Her focus on French poetry, particularly Raymond Queneau, and her involvement in projects like PRIMA « Le Rire des vers », highlight her interest in innovative ways to study textual and performative aspects of literature. Her publications demonstrate expertise in both theoretical aspects of computational literary studies and applied analysis, often through collaborative efforts.
